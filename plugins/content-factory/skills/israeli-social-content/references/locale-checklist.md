# Israeli localization checklist

Use this after the editorial and story decisions are approved.

- Preserve the original promise, proof, and audience.
- Use spoken Israeli Hebrew, short lines, and second-person benefit framing.
- Keep Latin tokens isolated from Hebrew where directionality can flip.
- Remove em dashes and invented slang.
- Treat Shabbat, holidays, August, and elections as timing hypotheses.
- Verify current platform policy from official documentation before making a
  claim about labels, limits, reach, or distribution.
- Use account analytics for timing and format decisions. Never replace them
  with a universal posting rule.
- Test the final copy in the target composer or rendered pixels.

The companion checker catches mechanical regressions. It cannot judge voice,
cultural sensitivity, or whether a claim is currently true.
