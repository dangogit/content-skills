# Formats

Pick the format that fits the message, not the one that's trendy. Each has a
native hook style, structure, and failure mode. All assume vertical 9:16 with
on-screen captions unless noted.

## Talking head
- **For:** opinions, education, POV, authority, story.
- **Hook:** bold/contrarian claim or curiosity gap, said to camera.
- **Structure:** claim → reason/evidence → payoff → CTA. Change framing when beat
  changes or visual proof adds information.
- **Length:** only as long as story and proof require. Compare variants.
- **Failure mode:** monotone delivery, no visual change, burying the point.

## Demo
- **For:** showing a product/result in action.
- **Hook:** the result or the problem, shown immediately (in media res).
- **Structure:** problem/result tease → product in action → transformation → CTA.
  Show, don't narrate. The visual carries it.
- **Length:** story-dependent; compare concise and fuller cuts.
- **Failure mode:** explaining before showing; slow setup.

## Unboxing
- **For:** new product reveal, anticipation.
- **Hook:** tease the thing without fully revealing it; withhold the brand.
- **Structure:** anticipation → reveal beats → first impression/result → CTA.
- **Length:** story-dependent; reveal without padding.
- **Failure mode:** no tension; revealing everything in the first second.

## Before / after
- **For:** transformations of any kind.
- **Hook:** flash the "after" first, then rewind to "before" (in media res).
- **Structure:** after tease → before → process (fast) → after (full) → CTA.
- **Length:** story-dependent; retain enough process to make the change credible.
- **Failure mode:** weak/unclear contrast; process too long.

## Tutorial / how-to
- **For:** teaching a repeatable result.
- **Hook:** promise the outcome + ease ("…in under a minute, no [pain]").
- **Structure:** outcome promise → numbered steps (on-screen) → result → CTA.
- **Length:** long enough to complete the promised steps clearly.
- **Failure mode:** too many steps, no payoff shown, assuming context.

## Storytime
- **For:** narrative, relatability, emotional pull.
- **Hook:** tease the ending or the stakes up front ("…and it cost me everything").
- **Structure:** open loop → rising tension → turn → resolution that closes the
  loop → takeaway/CTA. Use "but / therefore" between beats, never "and then."
- **Length:** long enough to close the narrative loop without filler.
- **Failure mode:** meandering; resolving the loop too early.

## Listicle
- **For:** dense value, saveable reference.
- **Hook:** the number + the promise ("5 [things] that [outcome]").
- **Structure:** hook → distinct items with on-screen counters → clear payoff →
  CTA. Test item order against completion and saves.
- **Length:** item- and proof-dependent.
- **Failure mode:** weak items padding the list; no payoff item.

## Carousel (static, IG/LinkedIn)
- **For:** education and saves where motion isn't needed.
- **Hook:** slide 1 is a pure hook (big text, one idea). See
  `assets/carousel-outline.md`.
- **Structure:** slide 1 hook → one idea per slide → second-to-last = summary →
  last = CTA. Each slide must earn the swipe to the next.
- **Length:** platform limit and message-dependent; use the fewest slides that
  preserve proof and payoff.
- **Failure mode:** burying the hook; multiple ideas per slide; no CTA.

## Meme / relatable
- **For:** reach and shares via recognition ("it me").
- **Hook:** the relatable tension stated flatly; text-on-screen does the work.
- **Structure:** setup → punchline/twist. Keep it tight; trust the format.
- **Length:** setup- and punchline-dependent.
- **Failure mode:** over-explaining the joke; forcing a product in.

## "You're doing it wrong"
- **For:** myth-busting, corrections, fast authority.
- **Hook:** name the common mistake as a stake ("Stop [common habit]. It's why
  [bad outcome]").
- **Structure:** the wrong way → why it fails → the right way → CTA.
- **Length:** explanation- and proof-dependent.
- **Failure mode:** scolding with no payoff; never showing the fix.

## POV
- **For:** relatability and immersion through a first-person scenario.
- **Hook:** drop the viewer straight into the moment ("POV: it's 6pm and …").
- **Structure:** set the scene → escalate the tension → land the relatable turn.
- **Length:** scenario-dependent.
- **Failure mode:** a POV too generic to feel like "me."

## Finding a distinct angle
Combine current audience pain, the creator's proof, and underused story role. Novelty
without authority or relevance is not a strategy.
