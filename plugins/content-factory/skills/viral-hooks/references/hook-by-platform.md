# Hooks by platform

Archetype may travel; execution does not. Verify current official documentation
for format, safe zones, labels, title fields, and media limits. Use account data
for pacing, opening duration, text density, and creative selection.

## Shared review

- First frame communicates topic, conflict, proof, or stake at phone scale.
- Spoken opening sounds natural and matches visible promise.
- On-screen text is readable, RTL-safe, and outside current UI overlays.
- Hook pays off. No unsupported number, authority claim, or false urgency.
- One main variable changes between test and baseline.

## TikTok

- Check current TikTok media and AI-label rules before handoff.
- Treat native font, trending sound, fast cuts, story openings, and text length as
  creative hypotheses. None guarantees distribution.
- Compare qualified views, watch behavior, and target action against account's
  same-format baseline.

## Instagram Reels

- Inspect first frame in feed and Reel surfaces; safe areas differ.
- Compare three-second view rate, watch time, saves, shares, and target action
  against same-format baseline. Do not use universal breakout thresholds.
- Avoid watermarks and recycled exports when current platform policy or quality
  review flags them.

## YouTube Shorts

- Ensure title and opening promise agree.
- Compare Viewed vs Swiped Away and retention against channel baseline.
- Verify current Shorts classification and music/Content ID rules from YouTube.
- Do not require `#Shorts` unless current platform documentation changes.

## Long-form YouTube

- Thumbnail/title create expectation; cold open validates it.
- Opening window depends on video length and viewer intent. No fixed 15, 30, or
  60-second rule.
- Inspect retention curve before moving sponsor, logo, or context beats.

## Paid social

- Match hook to funnel awareness and offer.
- Verify ad-platform policy, substantiation, AI disclosure, and landing-page match.
- Treat direct callout, confession, PAS, or bold claim as testable options.

## Cross-platform handoff

For each network record:

- current source URL and checked date;
- media/title/label requirements;
- UI safe-zone treatment;
- account baseline;
- test variable;
- unresolved policy or proof risk.
